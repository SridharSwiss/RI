The Integrated Analytics Stack Advantage

Our existing technology creates a powerful synergy. Each component plays a distinct, complementary role in realizing our vision of an intelligence-driven claims function.

The Data Intelligence Platform

Serves as the foundational layer. It ingests, stores, processes, and governs all claims-related data at scale, housing the Medallion Architecture. It's the engine for building and deploying all AI/ML models, from severity prediction to fraud detection.

The Business Intelligence Layer

The primary interface for business users. It provides intuitive, interactive dashboards and reports, democratizing data and enabling self-service analytics to foster a data-driven culture across the claims organization.

The Advanced Investigation Workbench

The specialized tool for deep-dive, complex investigations. It fuses disparate data sources to perform sophisticated network analysis, making it indispensable for tackling organized fraud and managing large-scale catastrophe events.

100-Day Action Plan

A phased approach to build momentum, deliver early value, and establish data-driven leadership within the first three months.

Phase I (Days 1-30): Discovery & Alignment

The primary objective is to listen, learn, and map the human, process, and technical landscape to build the foundation for change.

  • Stakeholder Mapping: Identify and engage key individuals to understand priorities and pain points.
  • Process Immersion: Shadow claims adjusters to map the end-to-end claims lifecycle and identify bottlenecks.
  • Tech Stack Evaluation: Assess the current state and maturity of Databricks, Power BI, and Palantir.

Phase II (Days 31-60): Strategy & Quick-Wins

Synthesize findings into a coherent strategy and identify a high-impact, low-effort "quick-win" project to demonstrate value.

  • KPI Definition Workshop: Gain consensus on a core set of measurable business outcomes.
  • Use Case Prioritization: Brainstorm and score potential use cases to identify the best quick-win candidate.
  • Develop a Prototype: Create a tangible mockup of the quick-win project to gather early feedback.

Phase III (Days 61-100): Execution & Value Demonstration

Deliver the quick-win project, demonstrate its value, and use this success to gain buy-in for the long-term strategic roadmap.

  • Deliver Quick-Win Project: Execute the end-to-end development of the prioritized project, such as the "Cedent Data Quality Dashboard".
  • Value Socialization: Craft a compelling data story to showcase the business outcomes and value delivered.
  • Roadmap Presentation: Present the full, multi-year strategic plan to senior leadership to secure sponsorship.

The End-to-End Data Flow

Follow a claim's journey from a raw cedent file to an actionable insight, showcasing the seamless integration of our technology stack.

1

Ingestion

Cedent submits PDF loss notice and Excel bordereau to cloud storage.

2

ETL & Cleansing (Databricks)

Auto Loader ingests files. Delta Live Tables pipeline uses OCR to parse the PDF, cleanses the data, and loads it into conformed Silver layer tables.

3

AI/ML Scoring (Databricks)

A pre-trained ML model scores the new claim in real-time, flagging it with a high probability of litigation. Aggregated Gold tables are updated.

4

BI Alert & Visualization (Power BI)

The Claims Manager's dashboard refreshes, flashing a "High Litigation Risk" alert. The manager drills through to a detailed report.

5

Deep Investigation (Palantir)

The high-risk claim is pushed to Palantir. An investigator uses graph analytics to uncover a network of related suspicious claims from the same attorney, enabling a proactive legal strategy.
